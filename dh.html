DebtHub - World Bank Data Visualization App January 2016

The aim of the project was to provide a tool to consult, visualize and export country debt data from the World Bank repositories focusing to get a better understanding of the social and economic context as well as the current debt situation of the world economies.

The app has two main parts: 1) A Info panel on the top right-side of the screen that updates when hoovering over the map and displays information about the so-called World Development Indicators, such as Surface Area, Total Population, Income Level, Gross Domestic Product (GDP), Life Expectancy and so on, that offer a first glance of the country background we want to consult. 2) A sidebar with four different tabs. When clicking on a country (e.g. Spain) a chart is created on the first tab displaying comparison values about Central Government Debt, Government Expenditure on Education and Health Expenditure (all in % of GDP). The charts are available to export in different formats. The other sections contain information about the project and the organization. Last tab is a simple contact form to report issues, bugs or any comments.
